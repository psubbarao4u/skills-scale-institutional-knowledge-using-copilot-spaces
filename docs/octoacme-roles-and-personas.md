# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Product Owner

### Role Summary
Product Owners guide product direction, set priorities, and ensure stakeholder needs are heard. They serve as the voice of the customer and bridge the gap between business stakeholders and the development team.

### Responsibilities
- Define and communicate the product vision and strategy
- Own and prioritize the product backlog based on business value
- Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria for the team
- Make decisions on feature scope and trade-offs
- Validate that delivered features meet stakeholder expectations
- Collaborate with [Project Managers](#project-managers) on release planning

### Goals
- Maximize business value delivered by the team
- Ensure stakeholder alignment on product direction
- Maintain a well-groomed, prioritized backlog
- Enable fast feedback loops between users and the team

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- Stakeholder demos and feedback sessions
- Collaboration with [Product Managers](#product-managers) on roadmap alignment
- Direct engagement with [Developers](#developers) during sprint execution

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works closely with **Product Managers** to align product vision with customer research and metrics
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** on sprint capacity and timeline commitments
- Engages **Stakeholders** to gather requirements and validate solutions
- Consults **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)** for domain-specific input on feature decisions

---

## Stakeholder

### Role Summary
Stakeholders bring business or user requirements to the project and participate in validation and prioritization discussions. They represent the interests of end-users, customers, or business units impacted by the project.

### Responsibilities
- Provide input on business needs, constraints, and expectations
- Participate in planning and review ceremonies when invited
- Validate that deliverables meet business requirements
- Escalate concerns or blockers that impact business outcomes
- Champion the project within their organization or department

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes align with business objectives
- Provide timely feedback to keep the project on track
- Maintain clear communication of evolving requirements
- Support adoption of delivered solutions within their domain

### Typical Communication
- Participation in sprint reviews and demos
- Feedback sessions with [Product Owners](#product-owner) and [Product Managers](#product-managers)
- Status updates from [Project Managers](#project-managers)
- Ad-hoc consultations on requirements clarification

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Collaborates with **Product Owners** to define and validate requirements
- Receives status updates and risk communications from **Project Managers**
- Provides domain context to **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)** and **Developers**
- Works with **Change Managers** during process or scope transitions

---

## Subject Matter Expert (SME)

### Role Summary
Subject Matter Experts provide specialized expertise and assist in technical or domain-specific decisions. They are consulted when deep knowledge is required to solve complex problems or validate solutions.

### Responsibilities
- Provide expert guidance on specialized technical or domain topics
- Assist in solution design and architecture decisions
- Review deliverables for compliance with domain standards
- Support knowledge transfer to team members
- Document key decisions and rationale for future reference

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet technical and domain requirements
- Share knowledge to build team capability
- Reduce risk by identifying domain-specific issues early
- Enable informed decision-making across the team

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with [Developers](#developers)
- Consultation meetings with [Product Owners](#product-owner) and [Product Managers](#product-managers)
- Documentation contributions and knowledge-sharing sessions
- Ad-hoc support during complex implementation phases

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Advises **Developers** on technical or domain-specific implementation details
- Supports **Product Owners** and **Product Managers** with feasibility assessments
- Collaborates with **Project Managers** to identify and mitigate technical risks
- Provides expertise to **Change Managers** during impact assessments

---

## Change Manager

### Role Summary
Change Managers coordinate process or scope changes, act as the liaison for change communication, and manage impact assessment. They ensure that all roles are informed of updates and that transitions are handled smoothly.

### Responsibilities
- Coordinate and document change requests and approvals
- Assess impact of proposed changes on scope, timeline, and resources
- Facilitate communication during transitions and process updates
- Ensure all affected roles are informed and prepared for changes
- Maintain change logs and audit trails for traceability
- Support [Project Managers](#project-managers) in risk mitigation related to changes

### Goals
- Minimize disruption from changes to project scope or process
- Ensure clear, timely communication of all changes
- Maintain traceability and documentation of change decisions
- Enable smooth transitions with minimal impact on delivery

### Typical Communication
- Change request meetings and approval workflows
- Impact assessment reports shared with [Project Managers](#project-managers) and [Stakeholders](#stakeholder)
- Transition briefings for all affected team members
- Regular updates to change logs and decision registers

### Interactions with Other Roles
- Works with **Project Managers** to assess and communicate change impacts
- Coordinates with **Product Owners** and **Product Managers** on scope changes
- Informs **Developers** and **SMEs** of technical or process changes
- Keeps **Stakeholders** updated on changes affecting business outcomes
